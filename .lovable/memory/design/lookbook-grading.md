All `/about` and brand editorial photography (canonical refs: `about-independence`, `about-professionals`) follows ONE grade:

- **Foreground is cool / neutral grey** — concrete, raw steel, charcoal cladding, dark glass. Subject's face and tee must read NEUTRAL.
- **Warm amber light is BACKLIGHT ONLY** — a narrow band on the far horizon, a rim through a doorway, a glint on hairline/shoulder. Never an overall wash.
- **Overall grade is muted, slightly cool, near-desaturated**, with a single small zone of warm amber as accent.
- **Wardrobe:** charcoal heather REPS performance tee. Pure-white REPS left-chest embroidery from `src/assets/brand/logo.svg`, exact letterforms.
- **Finish:** shallow DoF, soft 35mm grain, no graphic overlays, no captions, no extra logos.

**Reject any frame where the face or tee is amber-tinted ("tangoed").** That is the failure mode — warm light bleeding from backlight into a full golden-hour wash. Re-prompt with explicit "cool neutral grey foreground, warm light only as backlight/rim/horizon, subject's face and tee must read neutral, NOT amber".
